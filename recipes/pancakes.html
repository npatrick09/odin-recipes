Buttermilk Pancakes

Home

Servings: 2-3

Cook time: 20 minutes

Description

There’s nothing quite like the smell of warm, buttery pancakes in the morning—and these buttermilk pancakes deliver just that with barely any fuss. Made with basic pantry ingredients, this recipe yields a stack of fluffy, golden pancakes that are crisp at the edges and soft in the center.

Perfect for lazy weekends or quick weekday breakfasts, these pancakes come together in minutes and taste like a treat. Add your favorite toppings—like maple syrup, berries, or a pat of butter—for a cozy, satisfying start to your day.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter (plus more for cooking)

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, beat the egg, then mix in the buttermilk and melted butter.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. The batter should be slightly lumpy; don’t overmix.
  4. Heat a lightly buttered skillet or nonstick pan over medium heat. Pour about 1/4 cup of batter per pancake onto the skillet.
  5. Cook for 2–3 minutes, or until bubbles form on the surface and the edges look set. Flip and cook for another 1–2 minutes until golden brown and cooked through.
  6. Serve warm with butter, syrup, or your favorite toppings.
